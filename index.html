About Julius

Julius, Esq., is an orange tabby defense attorney for cats only. Calm and meticulous, he wins cases on evidence—nap logs, scratch-maps, and laser-pointer trajectories. He specializes in counter-surfing, plant mishaps, and keyboard walk-bys. Sliding-scale treats accepted; shelters pro bono. Courteous in court, devastatingly persuasive. Motto: “Nine lives. One fair defense.”

State v. Whiskers — “The Plant Was Already Dying”

Holding: Damage to a houseplant deemed pre-existing; Whiskers acquitted.

Precedent: Owners must prove plant vitality before alleging “malicious batting.”

Holding: Keyboard walking during Zoom was expressive conduct, not disruption.

Precedent: Enrichment plan; hotkeys remapped to “do nothing.”

City v. Noodle — “Right to Sunbeam Access”

Precedent: Curtains may be drawn only for compelling reasons (e.g., hail).
